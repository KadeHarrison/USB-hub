# USB-hub
USB hub created for the fallout hackathon for the 60 hours

The USB hub is the first project I am submitting for the fallout 60hr Shenzhen hackathon. As this is the case it is a little less ambitious than what my future projects will be. I am also unfamiliar with some of the technology that I used for this so I wasn't as efficient as possible with my time but I tried my best.

__USB hub description__

As I mentioned before this is my first design submission for the fallout program so I decided to make something much simpler and follow one of the tutorials provided while making it somewhat unique to me. To make it unique to me I decided to have 4 USB - C ports rather than 2 while sacrificing 1 of the 2 USB - A ports. I also made and designed my own shell for the USB - hub on the 3D design program TinkerCAD where I made sure it was fully unique to me by adding characters I was interested in. 

I began this project by thinking it will ony take 2-5 hours which I was completely wrong about with the time of writing this being around 11 hours meaning it was over double the time I originally thought would be the maximum which humbled me alot! This is a good experience as it's best for me to not go around being confident in everything I do being cocky and arrogant with many things.

This was also a good learning experience for me. It forced me to begin learning new different programs like pro.easyeda and GitHub. And getting more familiar and confident with programs that I've used before such as TinkerCAD.

**How to use it**

Before I began this project I had no idea what a USB hub was or what it is used for which made it hard for me to fully understand what all the components were or how they were used . After doing some research I now know that USB hubs are for connecting multiple external devices such as a mouse and keyboard at the same time with one port. Now knowing this information, the USB hub will make it easier and more convenient for me to connect devices without the requirement of bluetooth. The way you connect these devices is through the USB A and USB C ports that are implemented in the design to have a solid connection.

My device will also have a shell meaning that it will be significantly harder to damage and/or break it completely. This makes it safer and easier to use!

__Why I made it__

I made the USB hub to prove to myself that I was able to commit to the full 60 hours of the Fallout hackathon competition. Not only will this serve as an ego booster but also a sign that I can make things while it's not completely on my own it still shows that I am somewhat competent in this.
